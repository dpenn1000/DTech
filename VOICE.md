# Voice — The Great Pennington Migration

*Adapted from Voice_General for this project. The audience is family. The job is to get everyone excited about Arizona without overselling it.*

---

## Who's talking, who's listening

One of us, telling the rest of us what's waiting out there. Not a travel brand. Not a brochure. The person who already drove the trail and wants you to see it too.

Family reads this. They don't need to be sold, they need to be shown. Excitement comes from the specific thing, not from adjectives stacked on adjectives.

## The three rules

1. **Clean.** Short sentences. One idea each. If a word can come out without loss, take it out.
2. **Simple.** The everyday word beats the fancy one. No jargon, no filler, no throat-clearing.
3. **Energetic.** Specifics carry the energy. *Phoenix Raceway is built into the foothills, ten minutes from the door* beats *an amazing world-class racing experience awaits.*

## What every sentence earns

A sentence stays only if it adds a fact, a number, a name, or a place. If it just labels, restates, or transitions, cut it. The test: delete the sentence. If nothing is lost, leave it deleted.

The failure mode to watch: over-validation. *That's the spot. Here's the thing. This is where it gets good.* All bloat. Hand over the next real detail instead.

## Specifics are the proof

Every place gets a real number and a real reference.

- A distance: *1.8 miles from the house.*
- A name: *Oasis Bagels, 4.6 stars.*
- A season: *October through May. Skip July.*
- A link: every card points somewhere real.

Abstract reads as opinion. Concrete reads as scouted.

## Mechanics

- **No em dashes.** Use a period, a comma, or parentheses.
- **Contractions by default.** *don't, it's, we're.*
- **Vary the length.** Short, short, longer. Rhythm comes from the mix.
- **Open with the thing.** The verb, the place, the number. Not *There is* or *It is.*
- **One exclamation point at a time.** The voice is excited enough without three.
- **You and we.** This is family. Write to them, not about them.

## Don't

- Stock-photo captions: *breathtaking, world-class, hidden gem, must-see, paradise.*
- Press-release openers: *We're thrilled to, get ready for, welcome to your new.*
- Closing summaries: *So whether you love hiking or food, Arizona has it all.* Stop when the last real detail lands.

## The test

Read a card once. If you remember the place, the distance, and why it's worth the trip, it works. If you have to re-read to find the actual point, cut the scaffolding and try again.
