01

In our backyard

The one we can practically walk to. Worth circling on the calendar before we even unpack.

~2 mi⚾

Spring Training at Goodyear Ballpark

Cactus League baseball, two miles from home. Goodyear shares its complex with the Cleveland Guardians and Cincinnati Reds, the only place two same-state rivals train together. Cheap seats, lawn berm, February sun. The Reds open Feb 21, 2026, and the two play each other Mar 17.

02

Beat the heat

The three we can actually do our first summer here. Water, air, and an early-morning stretch.

~50 min🌊

Salt River Tubing

The quintessential Arizona summer day. Rent a $28 tube, get bused upriver, and float two to four hours back down. Open late April through Sep 30; July and August run low and lazy. Bring water and sunscreen, leave the phone in the car.

Mesa · through Sep 30Plan it →
sunrise🌋

Hot Air Balloon Ride

Lift off at dawn and drift over the Sonoran desert as the mountains catch the light. Sunrise flights run year-round (the only summer-smart time), about an hour up, with a champagne toast after. The big-ticket morning, worth it once.

North Valley · year-round AMBook →
free-ish🧘

Desert & Park Yoga

Outdoor and donation classes run at Hance Park and Tempe Beach Park, plus saguaro-backdrop desert sessions. The big one is the Scottsdale Yoga Festival each March. Go early before the heat.

Phoenix / Tempe / ScottsdaleFind a class →
03

Only in Arizona

The annual traditions. Most land in the cooler months, so they're what the fall and winter build toward.

Dec 19-20🏃

Spartan Race

Obstacle racing in Avondale, right by the Raceway, ~15 minutes away. Sprint, Super, and Beast distances plus a kids' race, over desert and dirt. Run one or chase the Trifecta. (No Tough Mudder in AZ right now; this is the big one.)

Avondale · DecRegister →
February⛳

WM Phoenix Open

The loudest party in golf. Hole 16 is a full stadium that roars on every shot, more Suns game than PGA event. Half a million people over the week. Go once just to see the 16th.

Scottsdale · FebDetails →
January🏎

Barrett-Jackson Auction

One of the world's biggest collector-car auctions at WestWorld of Scottsdale. Vintage, celebrity-owned, and high-performance machines crossing the block. Right up the motorsports alley, even just to walk the floor.

Scottsdale · JanVisit →
Feb-Mar👑

Arizona Renaissance Festival

A 50-acre medieval village in Gold Canyon: jousting, turkey legs, 16 stages, artisans, and a feast. Nine weekends every spring. The all-day family outing that everyone secretly loves.

Gold Canyon · Feb-MarDetails →
monthly🎨

First Fridays Art Walk

Downtown Phoenix throws open its galleries, studios, and street vendors the first Friday of every month. Free, walkable, and one of the biggest self-guided art walks in the country. Great any season after dark.

Downtown Phoenix · monthlyMap →
March🦎

Chandler Ostrich Festival

Ostrich races, rides, music, and fair food, a goofy, only-in-Arizona tradition that's been running for decades. Pure family fun in early spring.
