01

The trail starts at the house

Walk out the front door, around the end of the cul-de-sac, and you're on dirt. No driving anywhere first.

Red: the home at the cul-de-sac's end. Purple: the trail access, dozens of logged runs fanning into the desert.
Two ways out. North (blue): a flat half-mile wash to the lakes. South/east (clay): past the Bike Park, then east into open desert toward the Regional Park.

The path goes two ways from the house. North, a flat half-mile gets you to the two lakes, easy enough for anyone, kids and grandparents included. South and east, it heads into open desert and climbs toward the mountains for the people who want a real ride or hike.

That eastern stretch keeps going, all the way out toward the 19,840-acre Estrella Mountain Regional Park. So when you come visit, there's a lazy lake stroll out one direction and a proper desert adventure out the other. Same front door.

03

Pick your distance

Four outings on foot, from an easy lake lap to a real desert effort. Round-trip from the cul-de-sac.

2miles

The Lakes Lap

Flat · paved49 ft gain★★★★★4.7 / 481 reviews

A few minutes north on the wash path, then a loop around both lakes on smooth paved walkway. Ducks, water, mountain backdrop. Stroller- and dog-friendly, and the single most-used route in the community.

3miles

Lakes + Spine Out-and-Back

Flat · mixed surfaceEasy

The lakes lap plus a push along the community spine before turning back. Flat and forgiving, enough to stretch a 20-minute jog to a half hour. Shade pockets at the lake parks if you start late.

4miles

Desert Connector Loop

Rolling · dirtEasy–moderate

South off the cul-de-sac into the open-desert connectors, looping back through neighborhood paths. Saguaro, washes, quiet. The transition run when the lakes feel too tame.

5+ mi

Blackjack via Estrella Foothills

5.8 mi · 636 ft gainModerate★★★★½4.5 / 515 reviews

Rugged Sonoran terrain, rocky climbs, sweeping valley views. Trails intersect constantly, so download the map. The weekend objective: a desert mountain hike with no car.

04

Roll out the door and keep going

From the cul-de-sac: warm up on glassy lake pavement, drop into singletrack a reviewer calls "Disneyland for your mountain bike," then point east into open desert until the mountains swallow you. Five rides, each bigger than the last.

The warm-up · paved · easy

The Lake Spin

Glass-smooth pavement around both lakes, flat and fast. Coffee at the marketplace, a lap with the kids, a sunrise easy-day. Straight from the garage.

From the door · any bike, any age
The playground · ~1.5 mi south

FINS Singletrack

Fantasy Island Northern Singletrack: berms, flow, technical features, structures. One rider's verdict, "like Disneyland for your mountain bike." 4.7 stars. A short pedal, then hours of riding.

Beginner flow to expert lines
The skills lab · free

Foothills Bike Park

Public pump track and jump lines, beginner through advanced. Sharpen handling, let the kids session for hours. Sits on the eastern trail's doorstep, so it doubles as the launch pad.

Minutes away · free · all levels
The main event · ~7 mi · the east arm

Estrella Foothills Loop

Past the Bike Park, then east into raw Sonoran desert: "desert riding at its finest." Climbs exposed faces to bench-spots over the whole valley, past the landmark riders call the Circle of Trust, finishing with 3-foot+ jumps if you've got the speed. Forks everywhere, so it's a route you learn and own.

Moderate–technical · ride it tubeless
The expedition · all day · open-ended

Into the Backcountry

Where the Foothills Loop forks deeper, the desert opens into a choose-your-distance day stretching toward the 19,840-acre Estrella Mountain Regional Park and its 33 miles of trail. Unsigned, exposed, real.

From the door · expert · bring everything

The deep desert routes are informal, unmarked, and fork constantly. Load a GPS track (MTB Project and Trailforks both map this network), carry more water than feels reasonable, and ride the big stuff October through April.

Summer: dawn or skip it. Mid-70s before sunrise are runnable; by 9 AM it's done. Carry more water than you think, the desert trails have almost no shade. The lake parks have shade shelters if you need to bail. October to May is the payoff: 60s and 70s, blue sky, trails busy with the runners and riders who made those heat maps glow.
